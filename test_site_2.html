Nathaniel K. Mullin

I am an MD/PhD candidate at the University of Iowa where I am co-supervised by Robert Mullins and Budd Tucker. I am associated with the Institute for Vision Research (IVR), the Medical Scientist Training Program (MSTP), and the Interdisciplinary Graduate Program in Genetics (IGPG).

My research focuses on mitochondrial genetic disease, specifically on mitochondrial variants that cause blindness. Experimental techniques I use to understand this class of disease include:

  • Single cell genomic studies of primary patient tissues (scRNAseq and scATACseq)
  • Modeling retinal development in the presence of mitochondrial mutations using patient-derived iPSCs
  • Testing the effect of mitochondrial variants on cellular phenotype using iPSC-derived retinal cells

Previously, I have worked on generating transplantable iPSC-derived cell therapy products, modeled hematapoeitc development in vitro, and studied aberrant cell signaling in the development of skin fibrosis.
